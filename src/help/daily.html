Daily Workflow

The daily report gives the pathologists a snapshot of today's workflow and workload.


Data Table:

The table shows the backlog cases that belong to the person logged in, if you are a pathologist. It lists the case number, specialty, subspecialty, procedure, main (or only) specimen description, number of specimens, number of blocks, number of slides, (workload value), date received, date routed, the route technologist, the receiving pathologist, the time alloted to this case, the time spent so far calculated in (business hours), and the percent of time (%) that has passed. The table is initially sorted by the % column, but you can change the sort order by clicking on the column header of your choice.

A case is tagged by a red background if the time passed since accession has exceeded the time alloted in the turnaround time setup table. Amber background indicates the case has reached 70-99% of its alloted time, and a green background indicates 0-69% of the alloted time has passed. The elapsed time is calculated in business hours.


Cases Workflow:

The bar and line chart at the bottom shows today's distribution of cases among all the pathologists by workload value. The units of both Y-axis' are minutes, whereby a full-day load is equivalent to 360 minutes. The left-side Y-axis shows the new cases routed today in the amber bar, and the finalized cases are in the green bar. The right-side Y-axis shows the backlog (line with red circles) also in number of minutes required to complete the cases. The pathologists are sorted by their backlog volume.
